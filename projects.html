FriendOS

FriendOS is an Online Co-Op Action RPG where balls fly and viruses die! Strike and batter your opponents in Cartoon Sports Combat, and create the virtual animal avatar of your dreams. Hang out or team up with others to protect the world of FriendOS from virus corruption.

Position: Game Programmer, Game Designer, Level Designer, Generalist

Duration: September 2024 - Present

Releasing in Early Access in 2026


Made with the Godot Engine, this is the first commercial project of our indie game company, Dog in the Machine. Due to our small team size, many of us have taken on a generalist role. Aspects of the project that I have worked on include:

  • Back-end multiplayer networking and infrastructure for a multiplayer 3D game, integrated with Steam's Steamworks API
  • Mechanics and content design and programming, including enemies, weapons, and mobility
  • General game infrastructure including save systems, settings, localization, controller support, and tooling
  • Level design and implementation in-engine of small and medium sized environments, ranging from proc-gen dungeon rooms to full overworld environments

It has been extremely difficult but also extremely gratifying to work on a game project from the ground up that we are fully in control of. Dog in the Machine (our game company) is made up of many previous developers and artists that I worked with on Toontown: Corporate Clash, and working on FriendOS has truly let us develop the type of game we've always wanted to make.

Toontown Rewritten

Position: Game Programmer, Game Designer

Duration: August 2021 - March 2022, July 2024 - October 2025


While working on the project, as part of the Game Programmer role, I was responsible for developing major features for future content updates in Python using the Panda3D game engine. Features and projects that I worked on under this role include:

  • Working with and finalizing the revamp of the game's turn-based battle system
  • Finalizing new quest systems and objectives as part of the "Sellbot Task Force" update
  • Many various polish and cleanliness projects as part of finalizing the "Sellbot Task Force" update
  • Various miscellaneous work on every facet of the "Sellbot Task Force" update
  • Various game refactors developed for future content sustainability including the game's quests, building interiors, enemies, and player progression

As part of the Game Designer role, I was also responsible for collecting, organizing, and presenting user feedback, designing new turn-based battle encounters, referring to and reflecting upon design decisions present in the origin game, Toontown Online, and acting upon user feedback to improve the overall user experience for our players.

Capstone Project: Robot Heaven

Position: Game Programmer, Designer, Producer

Duration: October 2025 - May 2026


Robot Heaven was my capstone project for the Creative Technologies - Game Design major at Illinois State University. As part of this project, I created a game from the ground up with a partner and brought the game to completion within the scope and time constraints allowed over the course of an academic year.

  • Full game programming, ranging from back-end infrastructure to content development and UI
  • Contributed to design details and scope conversations
  • Managed tasks and project timeline to ensure the project could be completed within the academic year
